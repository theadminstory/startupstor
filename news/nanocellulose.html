Instructions for cheap Mass production of Nanocellulose. Under 50 cents per pound total cost including equipment purchase and deprecation, raw materials, facility, and energy costs. It needs rewritten to comply with your format. It is being released as Creative Commons and is not yet peer reviewed. Citation of Author, Adam Daugherty founder of MeetSmartPeople.com is requested by not required. A breakthrough has been made in the low cost production of CNF (Cellulose Nano Fibers). It is ready to be scaled up to mass production and is entirely green, requiring the use of no chemicals or solvents. The process works with any plant based material, including low cost wastes such as sawdust, grass clippings, leaf waste, or used cardboard. These materials can be obtained for under 5 cents a pound or less than $115 a ton. “https://resource-recycling.com/recycling/2017/10/10/chinese-actions-tank-us-occ-prices/“ There are 2 production methods covered here, one for lab/household production and one for commercial mass production. The lab/household method for low volumes is as follows. Start with raw cellulose containing material. Minimum 30% recommended. Shred materials using blender or a machine like this. “https://m.alibaba.com/product/347682625/Factory-sale-wood-sawdust-chippers-hammer.html?s=p&spm=a2706.amp_showroom” Add water to shredded material. Press solution through a cloth or paper filter with 200 micron pore size, a coffee filter works. Water will carry particles that are smaller than 200 microns created by the shredding, out of the solution and into your container. Re-dilute solution with more water. Run solution through .1 or .02 micron filter like Sawer or LifeStraw. “https://sawyer.com/water-filtration” Solution will now be Cellulose nano fibers with contamination of water, nano hemicellulose, nano lignin, minerals, and possibly dyes. You can evaporate the water with sunlight or by boiling the solution. You can now study the nano mixture or use a multi-step centrifuge process to isolate nanocellulose or other nano components by density. The unrefined mixture should Have electrical resistance of between 200-1200 ohms for samples under 1 inch. Pure nanocellulose should have electrical resistance less than that of copper, and be cheaper to manufacture, more sustainable, and more ecological friendly. “https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007%2F978-3-642-45232-1_74” Mass production method. Use single screw extrusion machine with progressively smaller exit pore size. Recommended first pore size, 5 mm multi-hole exit plate, then 1 mm, then 200 micron, then 20 micron, then 20 micron with vermiculite places before exit nozzle to create nano fibers under 100 Nm in width. Alternatively you can try to create 100nm sized pores is steel exit plate, but technologies to do so are in infancy and are expensive and slow at drilling through thick pieces of steel. Repeat centrifugal process to isolate nano materials if needed. Applications like reinforced plastics and 2x4’s made of concrete reinforced by cnf’s should not require the centrifugal separation.
